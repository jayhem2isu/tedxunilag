Ted
TED is an annual event that brings together the brightest minds of our generation to share their ideas with a curated audience of open-minded individuals. Based on any number of subjects, these “TED Talks”, are then made available online for TEDx free so that their ideas may be spread throughout the world.
Tedx
TEDx was created in the spirit of TED's mission, "ideas worth spreading." The program is designed to give communities, organizations and individuals the opportunity to create TED-like experiences at the local level through their own event. Since the program’s creation, more than 10,000 TEDx events have spread throughout the world in places like Dubai, Tokyo, Antarctica, and, of course, Unilag. At TEDx events, a screening of TEDTalks videos -- or a combination of live presenters and TEDTalks videos -- sparks deep conversation and connections. TEDx events are fully planned and coordinated independently, on a community-by-community basis.
TedxUnilag
TEDxUnilag was created out of the belief in the importance of learning and passing knowledge in our immediate environment and the society a large, as well as a need to provide insight on addressing the problems facing our society and ways to overcome these problems as individuals and as a whole. The first TEDxUnilag event held in March 2015 with a desire to develop connections, raise awareness, collaborate, and most importantly, innovate and learn. As a volunteer-only organization, organisers, volunteers and speakers are not paid. Instead, we oﬀer a global community and platform for launching unique ideas. Speakers have included a spoken word artiste, a doctor, a designer, a financial and tax consultant, an author, musicians and more Our mission is to bring together leading thinkers and doers to share ideas that matter in and to any discipline. Our vision is to establish a platform that will educate entertain and provoke conversations that will continue long after this event is over; promote conversations and share ideas that will provide insight for individuals within the community and the community as a whole
